## The Structure

The Collective is a mutual aid society with a business model.

Mutual aid with a business model - members contribute what they can, take what they need, no scorekeeping.

We're not here to extract. We're here to build.

### Membership Model

Prepaid membership (monthly or annual)

Recommended contribution is 10% for those who can afford it

Members can work-trade if they don't have capital

If you don't feel like you got 3 times the value from your membership...we'll work for free till we get you there or your money back plus a fee for wasting your time

### Value Definition

3x value includes everything that matters to you, not just money

Tangible Value:
- Income earned from projects
- Ownership stakes in ventures
- Infrastructure access (market value of tools/platforms)
- Consulting hours (market rate)
- Healthcare/benefit savings
- Legal protection (insurance value)

Intangible Value:
- Peace of mind that you're not alone
- Strategic clarity on your next moves
- Network of people who actually get it
- Being positioned correctly before the cliff
- Having backup when disruption hits
- Community that gives a cuss
- Feeling like you're building the alternative instead of just complaining
- Time saved not spinning your wheels
- Confidence from having a plan
- The absence of anxiety about the future

Member-defined KPIs at onboarding - binary yes/no tracking of what success looks like for each individual

### Core Model - No Ownership

lets forget the ownership all together

No individual ownership percentages, no cap tables, no equity negotiations

No "I built this so I own X%"

Everything belongs to everyone. Everything benefits everyone.

Avatar licensing platform? Belongs to the Collective. Revenue goes back to fund more projects.

Silver tsunami roll-ups? Owned by the Collective. Profits fund more acquisitions.

AI validation lab? Collective-owned. Successful projects benefit everyone equally.

You don't own anything more than anyone else. But you made it possible.

### How Work Actually Works

Everyone just works on whatever needs doing.

No timesheets. No "I worked on Project X so I get Y% of Project X."

We're all working towards the same goal.

Communal work without scorekeeping.

Actor needs avatar platform built → whoever has skills builds it

Someone needs transition coaching → whoever can help, helps

Deal needs evaluation → whoever knows the domain, evaluates

Someone's in crisis → whoever can support them, does

You contribute what you can:
- Got capital? You funded the infrastructure
- Got skills? You build stuff
- Got time? You help others
- Got network? You make intros
- Got expertise? You advise

You take what you need:
- Need income? Work on paid projects
- Need coaching? Get coaching
- Need healthcare? Pool covers it
- Need emergency funds? Mutual aid kicks in
- Need a break? Take a break

### Accountability - Cultural Not Bureaucratic

Social norms regulate behavior naturally

Public celebration for over-contributors:
- "Sarah built the avatar platform this month, crushed it"
- Recognition matters more than monetary incentives
- Other members want to work with them
- They get first look at best opportunities

Voluntary 10% with social accountability:
- Not tracked bureaucratically
- But if someone is freeloading, there's public accountability
- Culture filters, not contracts

If someone starts taking way more than contributing:
- Month 1-3: No big deal, people need support sometimes
- Month 4-6: "Hey, noticed you've been taking a lot of value lately. Everything okay?"
- Month 7-9: Gentle conversation about balancing
- Month 10-12: If genuinely building something valuable - keep going. If just extracting - "this isn't working out"

People notice. We have conversations. It self-regulates.

Hasn't been a problem.

### High-Leverage Value Model

The math works because:

Not everyone is in crisis at once

High-leverage wins are massive and distributed

Network effects compound exponentially

Access > ownership for most things

Community support costs less than individual survival

Taylor Swift principle:
- One celebrity collaboration brings massive value
- One successful roll-up returns massive value
- One infrastructure build serves 1,000+ people
- One high-leverage win pays for 100+ low-leverage needs

The collective is designed to capture high-leverage wins and distribute them as support for everyone.

Edge cases where someone takes more than they contribute are rounding errors compared to structural wins.

Take what you need. Don't worry about it. In practice, it basically never becomes an issue because people are reasonable and the high-leverage wins dwarf any individual usage.

### Legal Structure

The Collective Mutual Benefit Corporation

Not a fund (no securities registration needed)

Not a consulting firm (though services provided)

A genuine mutual benefit organization

Members pay annual dues (like a credit union)

Dues fund collective activities:
- Project development
- Member employment
- Mutual aid programs
- Infrastructure building

Members participate in upside through:
- Participation in projects they work on
- Revenue sharing from successful infrastructure
- Access to opportunities before non-members
- Mutual aid when they need it

No securities issues because:
- Membership dues, not investment
- Mutual benefit structure (like co-op)
- Returns come from participation, not passive investment
- It's mutual aid, not securities offering

This is legally clean.
